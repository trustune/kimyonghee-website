## Research Overview

This study examines the evolution of platform power in Korea's home shopping industry, analyzing how asymmetric market structures create and sustain competitive advantages in the television commerce sector.

## Research Context

### Korean Home Shopping Market
- One of the world's most developed TV commerce markets
- Multiple competing platforms with varying market shares
- Rapid growth from 2015-2024 (0 to 2+ trillion KRW)
- Complex relationship with broadcasting ecosystem

### Platform Power Dynamics
- Market concentration patterns
- Bargaining power with suppliers
- Content distribution advantages
- Consumer lock-in effects

## Theoretical Framework

### Asymmetric Market Evolution
- **Network Effects**: Platform size advantages
- **Switching Costs**: Consumer retention mechanisms
- **Supply-side Economies**: Vendor relationship benefits
- **Information Asymmetries**: Data and analytics advantages

### Sustainability Dimensions
- Economic sustainability
- Social responsibility
- Environmental considerations
- Governance structures

## Methodology

### Data Collection
- Home shopping platform financial data
- Market share and transaction volumes
- Vendor and consumer surveys
- Regulatory and policy documents

### Analytical Techniques
- Market structure analysis
- Platform power metrics
- Sustainability performance assessment
- Comparative case studies

## Key Findings

### 1. Market Concentration Trends
- Increasing concentration among top platforms
- Barriers to entry for new competitors
- Scale advantages in content production
- Network effects reinforcing dominance

### 2. Platform Power Mechanisms
- **Vendor Relations**: Preferential terms for large platforms
- **Consumer Base**: Loyalty program effectiveness
- **Content Quality**: Production capability differences
- **Technology**: Advanced analytics and personalization

### 3. Sustainability Implications
- Economic: Revenue distribution fairness
- Social: Labor practices and vendor treatment
- Environmental: Logistics and packaging
- Governance: Transparency and accountability

### 4. Policy Challenges
- Market power regulation
- Fair competition maintenance
- Consumer protection
- Sustainability standards enforcement
