## Research Overview

This study introduces the "Regulatory Sandwich Effect" — a phenomenon where mid-sized platform companies are disproportionately affected by regulatory discussions compared to both large and small platforms.

## Key Findings

### Market Reactions
- Platform companies show significantly larger negative stock price reactions than IT service companies
- Heterogeneous effects by firm size (tier-based analysis)

### The Regulatory Sandwich Effect
- **Large platforms**: Limited negative reaction — sufficient market power, bargaining leverage, and capital to absorb regulatory costs
- **Mid-sized platforms**: Strongest negative impact — subject to regulation but lacking resources to manage it
- **Small platforms**: Less affected — often below regulatory thresholds

### Statistical Evidence
- ANOVA confirms significant tier differences at 5-10% level across all four policy events
- Nonlinear relationship between firm size and regulatory market impact

## Sample and Data

- **Platform companies**: 11 firms
- **IT service companies**: 6 firms (control group)
- **Policy events**: 4 major events (March 2024 – January 2025)
- **Method**: Event study with cumulative abnormal returns (CAR)

## Theoretical Contributions

1. Extends policy uncertainty theory to platform regulation context
2. Applies resource-based view to explain size-based heterogeneity
3. Introduces "Regulatory Sandwich Effect" as a new concept
4. Demonstrates nonlinear market impacts of regulation by firm size

## Policy Implications

- Regulatory design should consider differential impacts across firm sizes
- One-size-fits-all regulation may disproportionately harm mid-sized firms
- Broadcasting Development Fund levy expansion requires nuanced policy calibration
