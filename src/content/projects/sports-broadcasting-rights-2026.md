## Project Overview

This project analyzes the structural transformation of sports broadcasting rights markets, with a focus on the Korean market's transition from collective purchasing (Korea Pool) to competitive single-buyer acquisition. The analysis was developed for an invited expert presentation at JTBC, triggered by the 2026 Milano Winter Olympics broadcasting outcomes.

## The Core Question

When JTBC recorded a 1.9% household rating for the Milano Olympics opening ceremony — compared to KBS's 9.9% for Beijing 2022 — media coverage called it a "ratings disaster." But was it really failure, or structural transformation?

## Key Arguments

### 1. Global Market Transformation
- IOC broadcasting rights grew from $1.2M (1960 Rome) to $7.65B per cycle (2032 Brisbane)
- Player substitution: traditional broadcasters replaced by tech/streaming companies
- 2024 Paris Olympics: 60% of viewing in select markets occurred on streaming platforms

### 2. Korea Pool Dissolution
- 30-year collective purchasing system (SBS-led consortium) ended
- IOC preference for competitive bidding made collective bargaining untenable
- JTBC emerged as sole rights holder — structurally equivalent to NBC's position in the US market

### 3. Universal Access to Sports — Five Critical Objections
1. **Hold-up problem**: Forces rights holder into below-market sublicensing
2. **Regulatory capture risk**: Terrestrial broadcasters lobby for protection, not public interest
3. **Investment deterrence**: Reduces willingness to bid competitively
4. **Platform blindness**: Current law assumes terrestrial TV as sole delivery mechanism
5. **International precedent**: Only 6 countries have such laws; most rely on market mechanisms

### 4. The 2026 World Cup Opportunity
- 48-nation format with 104 matches (vs previous 32-nation, 64-match format)
- Three scenarios for JTBC: solo premium, consortium return, or hybrid OTT model
- Technology differentiation (multi-angle, interactive stats, AI highlights) as competitive advantage

## Context

This analysis was commissioned following the researcher's expert commentary in Digital Daily's investigative series on sports broadcasting rights (February-March 2026). The JTBC presentation covered the full landscape from regulatory economics to competitive strategy.
