# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

- **Academic peers** in media policy, digital platforms, AI governance, and data analytics who arrive to read publications, check citations, and gauge credibility.
- **Policymakers and industry readers** (broadcasting, telecom, OTT, advertising) who follow the policy briefs, project analyses, and the weekly MediaINT / JournalINT intelligence.
- **Students at Sunmoon University** using the teaching pages, course syllabi, and the data-analytics textbook chapters.
- **Journalists and general readers** following commentary on Korean media and technology policy.
- **Collaborators, conference organizers, and grant agencies** evaluating the body of work.

Context: bilingual audience (Korean primary, English secondary), reading on both desktop and mobile, often scanning for a specific paper, credential, or briefing rather than browsing linearly.

## Product Purpose

The canonical personal academic platform for Yonghee Kim (Assistant Professor, Sunmoon University). It presents research output (publications with live citation tracking, projects, policy briefs), teaching (courses and a data-analytics textbook), and ongoing media/journal intelligence (MediaINT and JournalINT weekly). It exists to establish scholarly authority and act as the single reference hub for his work. Success: a visitor grasps who he is and what he studies within seconds, finds the specific paper or credential they came for, and leaves trusting the substance of the content.

## Brand Personality

Warm and approachable, while remaining authoritative and precise. The voice is that of a teacher and expert who explains rather than impresses: clear, generous, human, never corporate. Three words: **warm, credible, lucid.** The warmth is in tone and craft, not in decoration.

## Anti-references

- **Generic AI / SaaS-generated look.** No purple-to-blue gradients, no repeated icon-card grids, no gradient text, no hero-metric template. If a visitor could say "AI made this," it has failed.
- **Flashy marketing landing pages.** No oversized hype headlines, no buzzwords (혁신 / 최적화 / streamline / empower / next-generation), no campaign-page theatrics. This is a scholar's site, not a product launch.
- **Critical:** warmth must NOT be produced by the cream / sand / beige warm-tinted near-white background that is the saturated AI default. Carry warmth through accent color, typography, and imagery, never through a beige canvas.

## Design Principles

1. **Teacher's clarity.** Explain, don't impress. Hierarchy and content guide the reader; on every screen the next step is obvious. This is where warmth and approachability live.
2. **Earned authority.** Credibility comes from substance shown plainly: real citations, real data, real findings. Never from visual hype.
3. **Warmth without the cliche.** Human warmth is carried by accent, type, and imagery, never by a beige/cream AI-default canvas. Distinctiveness is the bar for a brand register.
4. **Bilingual parity.** Korean and English are both first-class. Every layout must read well in both; no language is an afterthought.
5. **Restraint with a pulse.** Keep the minimal academic calm, but commit to one accent and to intentional motion so the site reads as crafted, not sterile.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. The site already ships a skip-nav link and visible focus outlines; hold that bar. Body text must meet >=4.5:1 contrast (large text >=3:1), honor `prefers-reduced-motion` with a non-motion alternative for every animation, and keep the bilingual `data-lang` switching screen-reader coherent.
